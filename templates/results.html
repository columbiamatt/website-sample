Maryland Election Administration Project Results

I set out to answer whether there is evidence that county election officials use performance data from one election to improve the next election. I was surprised to see how much stability there is in county election administration. It was rare for any county where I saw more than a 5 step move in either direction for the python-generated metrics of voters per polling place and voters per poll worker.


Maybe not surprisingly, smaller counties by voter registration tended to perform better relative to larger jurisdictions in Maryland. These counties also tend to be smaller geographically and are more homogenous. I was surprised by how low Montgomery County's election administration metircs ranked the county relative to other Maryland counties as I know the county to be relatively state of the art. It's possible that a burgeoning population and a highly interested electorate are resulting in more crowded than expected polling places and relatively frazzled poll workers.

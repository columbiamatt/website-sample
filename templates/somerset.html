Somerset County

Somerset County is the second smallest county or county-equivalent in the State of Maryland based on the 2014 registration data.


  • In 2012, Somerset county had 13,715 registered voters. By 2014, that number was 12,999.
  • In 2012, Somerset county's turnout of registered voters was 76.50% and in 2014 it was 52.30%. The statewide averages were 75.33% and 47.14%, respectively.
  • In 2012, the county had 589 voters per polling place, ranking 3rd out of 24 counties in this metric. In 2014, the county had 393 voters per polling place, ranking 2nd out of 24 counties. The statewide average of voters per polling place in 2012 was 1,264 and the median was 1,198. In 2014, the average voters per polling place statewide was 808 voters and the median was also 808 voters.
  • In 2012, the county had 51 voters per poll worker, ranking 2nd out of 24 counties in this metric. In 2014, the county had 56 voters per poll worker, ranking 9th out of 24 counties. The statewide average of voters per poll worker in 2012 was 88 voters and the median was 87 voters. In 2014, the average voters per poll worker statewide was 60 voters and the median was 61 voters.

Somerset may be a small jurisdiction but it ranks near the top of Maryland counties in both the voters per polling place and voters per poll worker metrics.

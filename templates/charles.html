Charles County

Charles County is the tenth largest county or county-equivalent in the State of Maryland based on the 2014 registration data.


  • In 2012, Charles county had 92,524 registered voters. By 2014, that number was 100,449.
  • In 2012, Charles county's turnout of registered voters was 81.98% and in 2014 it was 47.62%. The statewide averages were 75.33% and 47.14%, respectively.
  • In 2012, the county had 1,334 voters per polling place, ranking 17th out of 24 counties in this metric. In 2014, the county had 866 voters per polling place, ranking 15th out of 24 counties. The statewide average of voters per polling place in 2012 was 1,264 and the median was 1,198. In 2014, the average voters per polling place statewide was 808 voters and the median was also 808 voters.
  • In 2012, the county had 98 voters per poll worker, ranking 16th out of 24 counties in this metric. In 2014, the county had 69 voters per poll worker, ranking 18th out of 24 counties. The statewide average of voters per poll worker in 2012 was 88 voters and the median was 87 voters. In 2014, the average voters per poll worker statewide was 60 voters and the median was 61 voters.

Charles is an average county. Some of the density at polling places and strain on the poll workers could be due to the higher than state average turnout of its voters.
